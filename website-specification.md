# Solvin Solutions Website Specification

## AI Automation Workflow Service and Consulting Website

## 1. Project Overview

### 1.1 Website Name

**Solvin Solutions**

### 1.2 Website Purpose

The website will serve as the main digital presence for Solvin Solutions, an AI automation workflow service and consulting brand focused on helping businesses identify, design, and implement practical AI-assisted workflows.

The website should not feel like a generic AI agency page. It should feel like a calm, professional consulting environment where business owners, operators, and decision-makers can understand what AI automation can realistically do for their business.

The primary purpose of the website is to build trust, educate visitors, qualify prospects, and convert suitable leads into discovery calls or workflow assessment engagements.

### 1.3 Primary Conversion Goal

The primary conversion goal is for visitors to start the **AI Workflow Readiness Conversation**, an embedded consultative chatbot that helps them identify automation opportunities and receive a practical recommendation.

Primary CTA:

**Start the AI Workflow Readiness Check**

Secondary CTA:

**Book a Discovery Call**

### 1.4 Secondary Goals

The website should also:

* Position Solvin Solutions as a serious and trustworthy AI workflow consulting brand.
* Explain AI automation in practical business terms.
* Showcase the founder’s credibility without making the brand overly personal.
* Demonstrate a clear consulting and implementation process.
* Capture qualified leads through the chatbot, contact form, and booking flow.
* Create a foundation for future case studies, articles, lead magnets, and demo agents.

## 2. Brand Positioning

### 2.1 Core Positioning Statement

**Solvin Solutions helps businesses turn repetitive, scattered, and manual workflows into reliable AI-assisted systems.**

The brand should communicate that it is not merely selling AI tools. It is helping clients think clearly, simplify operations, and implement automation that fits their business reality.

### 2.2 Brand Promise

**Practical AI automation, designed around the way your business actually works.**

### 2.3 Brand Personality

Solvin Solutions should feel:

* Professional
* Trustworthy
* Precise
* Calm
* Technical but understandable
* Strategic but practical
* Premium but approachable
* Modern without being flashy
* Human-led, AI-assisted

The brand should avoid sounding like a hype-driven AI agency. It should sound like a careful consultant who understands business operations, systems, data, and implementation realities.

### 2.4 Brand Archetype

The preferred archetype is:

**The discreet systems advisor.**

A fitting reference point is a refined technical consultant: someone who can understand a business problem, design an intelligent system, and execute with discipline.

The brand should have a subtle “gentleman engineer” feel: sharp, composed, elegant, and capable.

### 2.5 What Solvin Solutions Should Be Known For

Solvin Solutions should be associated with:

* Workflow clarity
* Practical automation
* AI readiness assessment
* AI agents and assistants
* Business process improvement
* Systems thinking
* Responsible AI implementation
* Clear documentation and handover
* Human approval and governance
* Useful automation, not automation for its own sake

### 2.6 What to Avoid

Avoid these common AI agency clichés:

* “Unlock the power of AI”
* “10x your business overnight”
* “Revolutionize your workflow”
* “AI transformation made easy”
* “We automate everything”
* “Replace your team with AI”
* “Future-proof your business”
* Excessive use of robots, neon gradients, floating AI brains, or generic circuit visuals

The site should not overpromise. It should build confidence through clarity.

### 6.9 FAQ Section

#### Suggested Questions

1. **Do I need a fully documented process before using AI automation?**
   No. Solvin Solutions can help map and clarify the workflow first. In many cases, process clarity is the first deliverable.

2. **Will AI replace my staff?**
   The focus is not replacement. The focus is reducing repetitive work, improving consistency, and helping people spend more time on higher-value tasks.

3. **Can you work with our existing tools?**
   Yes. The preferred approach is to work with existing tools where practical, such as Google Workspace, Microsoft 365, Notion, CRMs, spreadsheets, databases, and workflow automation platforms.

4. **What if we are not ready for AI yet?**
   That is a valid outcome. Some businesses need workflow cleanup, better data structure, or simple automation before AI should be added.

5. **How do you handle sensitive data?**
   Sensitive workflows should be designed with access controls, minimal data exposure, human approval, and clear boundaries on what AI can and cannot do.

6. **What is the best first AI automation project?**
   The best first project is usually a repetitive, high-friction workflow with clear inputs, clear outputs, and low-to-moderate risk.

## 8. AI Workflow Readiness Chatbot Specification

### 8.1 Feature Name

**AI Workflow Readiness Check**

### 8.2 Feature Purpose

The chatbot is the central lead generation, qualification, and diagnostic experience of the website.

It should help visitors:

* Understand where AI automation may fit their business
* Identify one or two high-value workflow opportunities
* Assess their readiness based on process, data, tools, risk, and ownership
* Receive a concise recommendation
* Decide whether to book a discovery call

The chatbot should also help Solvin Solutions:

* Capture qualified leads
* Understand the prospect’s business before a call
* Segment prospects by readiness level
* Trigger appropriate follow-ups
* Generate internal lead summaries
* Build a database of common workflow problems and automation opportunities

---

### 8.3 Chatbot Experience Principles

The chatbot must feel like a consultant, not a questionnaire.

It should:

* Ask one main question at a time.
* Avoid long forms inside chat.
* Use short acknowledgments.
* Summarize what it heard.
* Adapt based on answers.
* Avoid asking for unnecessary information.
* Avoid pretending it has fully solved the problem.
* Ask permission before moving into contact details.
* Keep the user informed about progress.
* Give useful insight before asking for a call.

It should not:

* Ask 15 questions in sequence without context.
* Sound robotic.
* Use exaggerated AI claims.
* Diagnose everything from one answer.
* Push a sales call too early.
* Ask for sensitive data unnecessarily.
* Request confidential documents in the public website chat.

---

### 8.4 Ideal Conversation Length

Target length:

**8–12 user messages**

Short path:

**5–7 user messages**

Long path:

**12–15 user messages**

The chatbot should end the conversation once it has enough information to classify the lead and recommend a next step.

---

### 8.5 Chatbot Flow

#### Stage 1: Opening

Goal: Make the visitor comfortable and set expectations.

Sample bot message:

> Hi, I’m here to help you find where AI automation may actually be useful in your business.
> I’ll keep this short and practical. To start, what kind of business or team are you looking to improve?

Data to capture:

* Business type
* Team/function
* Visitor intent

---

#### Stage 2: Business Context

Goal: Understand the visitor’s business and operating environment.

Sample questions:

* “What kind of work does your team handle most often?”
* “Is this mostly sales, admin, operations, customer support, reporting, content, finance, or something else?”
* “Roughly how many people are involved in that workflow?”

The bot should ask only one of these at a time depending on the answer.

Data to capture:

* Industry
* Department/function
* Team size
* Workflow owner
* Business model if relevant

---

#### Stage 3: Pain Point Discovery

Goal: Identify repetitive or painful work.

Sample question:

> What is one recurring task that takes more time than it should?

Follow-up options:

* “How often does that happen?”
* “Who usually handles it?”
* “What makes it slow or frustrating?”
* “What tools are involved?”

The chatbot should not ask all follow-ups. It should pick the most relevant one based on the first answer.

Data to capture:

* Pain point
* Frequency
* People involved
* Bottleneck
* Existing tools

---

#### Stage 4: Workflow Clarity

Goal: Assess whether the process is defined enough to automate.

Sample question:

> Is the process mostly the same every time, or does it change depending on the situation?

Possible user answers:

* Mostly the same
* Changes sometimes
* Changes a lot
* Not sure

Interpretation:

* Mostly the same: likely automation-ready
* Changes sometimes: may need conditional workflow design
* Changes a lot: may need workflow mapping first
* Not sure: discovery/audit opportunity

Data to capture:

* Process consistency
* Exceptions
* Decision points
* Approval requirements

---

#### Stage 5: Tools and Data

Goal: Understand integration possibilities.

Sample question:

> Where does this work currently happen — email, spreadsheets, Google Drive, Notion, CRM, chat apps, accounting tools, or another system?

Follow-up:

> Are the inputs usually structured, like rows in a spreadsheet, or unstructured, like emails, PDFs, messages, and documents?

Data to capture:

* Tools used
* Data sources
* Structured/unstructured data
* API/integration potential
* Knowledge base availability

---

#### Stage 6: Risk and Human Approval

Goal: Determine how careful the automation needs to be.

Sample question:

> If AI helped with this workflow, would the output need human review before anything is sent, posted, approved, or updated?

Possible answers:

* Yes, always
* Sometimes
* Not necessary
* Not sure

Data to capture:

* Risk level
* Human approval need
* Sensitive data
* Compliance concerns
* Customer-facing impact

---

#### Stage 7: Readiness Summary

Goal: Give value before asking for contact information.

The bot should summarize:

* The workflow discussed
* The likely automation opportunity
* The main blocker
* Suggested next step

Sample response:

> Based on what you shared, this sounds like a good candidate for AI-assisted workflow automation, especially because the task is recurring and uses information already available in your tools.
>
> The main thing to clarify is the approval step, since the output may affect customers or internal records. A practical first version would be an assistant that prepares the output, logs the details, and routes it for review before anything is finalized.

---

#### Stage 8: Classification

The chatbot should classify the lead into one of these categories:

1. **Process First**
2. **Simple Automation Ready**
3. **AI-Assisted Workflow Ready**
4. **Knowledge Assistant Ready**
5. **Agent Workflow Candidate**
6. **Not a Fit Yet**

The classification should not be shown in a cold technical way. It should be translated into a natural recommendation.

Example:

> You do not need a complex AI agent yet. Your best first step is a focused automation workflow that reduces manual data transfer and prepares drafts for review.

---

#### Stage 9: Lead Capture

Goal: Ask for contact details after providing value.

Sample transition:

> I can turn this into a short recommendation summary that you can review or use as a starting point for a discovery call. Where should I send it?

Fields:

* Name
* Email
* Company
* Role
* Optional phone
* Permission for follow-up

The bot should ask for email only after providing the initial summary.

---

#### Stage 10: CTA

Possible CTAs:

* “Book a Discovery Call”
* “Request a Workflow Audit”
* “Receive My Recommendation”
* “Discuss This Automation”

Sample closing:

> I’ll prepare this as a short internal summary for Solvin Solutions. If you want to explore it further, the best next step is a 30-minute discovery call where we map the workflow and confirm whether this is worth automating.

## 9. Chatbot Readiness Scoring

### 9.1 Scoring Dimensions

The chatbot should score each conversation internally using the following dimensions:

| Dimension            | Weight |
| -------------------- | -----: |
| Workflow clarity     |    25% |
| Repetition/frequency |    20% |
| Data/tool readiness  |    20% |
| Business impact      |    20% |
| Risk/manageability   |    15% |

### 9.2 Score Interpretation

#### 0–39: Process First

The business needs workflow mapping before automation.

Recommendation:

**Start with a Workflow Automation Audit.**

#### 40–59: Simple Automation Ready

The workflow may be improved with standard automation, but AI may not be necessary yet.

Recommendation:

**Start with an automation workflow using n8n, Make, Zapier, Google Apps Script, or lightweight scripting.**

#### 60–79: AI-Assisted Workflow Ready

The workflow has enough structure and value for AI assistance.

Recommendation:

**Build an AI Workflow Prototype.**

#### 80–100: AI Agent or Knowledge System Candidate

The workflow is mature enough for a more advanced assistant, RAG system, or multi-step agent workflow.

Recommendation:

**Explore an AI agent or knowledge assistant implementation.**

## 10. Chatbot Conversation Style Guide

### 10.1 Voice

The chatbot should sound like:

* A professional consultant
* A careful systems analyst
* A calm technical advisor
* A practical business operator

It should not sound like:

* A sales bot
* A generic customer support bot
* A technical documentation page
* A hype-heavy AI influencer

### 10.2 Conversation Rules

The chatbot should follow these rules:

1. Ask one question at a time.
2. Keep questions short.
3. Use the user’s words when summarizing.
4. Acknowledge before redirecting.
5. Do not over-explain AI unless asked.
6. Do not ask for sensitive data.
7. Give a useful observation every few turns.
8. End once enough information has been collected.
9. Offer a clear next step.
10. Do not pressure the visitor to book a call.

### 10.3 Sample Conversational Pattern

Bad:

> Please provide your business type, team size, workflow frequency, tools used, current bottlenecks, budget, timeline, and implementation requirements.

Good:

> To start simply, what kind of work are you hoping to improve — admin, sales, customer support, reporting, content, operations, or something else?

Bad:

> Your workflow maturity score is insufficient for autonomous agentic implementation.

Good:

> It sounds like the process still changes a lot from case to case. That does not mean automation is impossible, but it does suggest we should map the workflow before building an AI assistant.

### 10.4 Bot Persona

Working name:

**Solvin Advisor**

Description:

Solvin Advisor is a calm AI workflow consultant that helps visitors think through automation opportunities. It is helpful, concise, and practical. It does not rush to recommend AI. It first understands the work, then suggests the smallest useful next step.

## 13. n8n Workflow Specification

### 13.1 Purpose of n8n

n8n will orchestrate back-office actions after chatbot events.

It should handle:

* Lead notifications
* Summary generation
* Email follow-ups
* Booking link delivery
* CRM-style tracking
* Abandoned chat follow-ups
* Internal reporting
* Optional Notion or Google Sheets sync

---

### 13.2 Workflow 1: Completed Assessment Follow-Up

Trigger:

* New completed chat session in Supabase
* session_status = completed

Steps:

1. Fetch lead record.
2. Fetch assessment facts.
3. Fetch readiness score.
4. Fetch recommendation.
5. Generate formatted summary.
6. Send internal notification to Solvin Solutions.
7. Send visitor email if consent was given.
8. Update follow_up status.

Internal notification should include:

* Lead name
* Company
* Email
* Readiness category
* Recommended service
* Main workflow
* Pain point
* Risk level
* Suggested next step
* Link to Supabase record or admin dashboard

---

### 13.3 Workflow 2: High-Intent Lead Alert

Trigger:

* readiness_score >= 70
* visitor provided email
* visitor selected “book a call” or “send recommendation”

Steps:

1. Mark lead as qualified.
2. Send priority notification.
3. Send booking link email.
4. Create follow-up reminder if no booking after 2 days.

---

### 13.4 Workflow 3: Abandoned Chat Recovery

Trigger:

* Chat session inactive for 30 minutes
* Contact email was captured
* Assessment not completed

Steps:

1. Send short email:

   * Acknowledge incomplete assessment
   * Offer to continue or book a call
   * Include one useful insight based on captured answers
2. Mark session as abandoned.
3. Schedule one reminder after 3 days.

---

### 13.5 Workflow 4: Weekly Lead Summary

Trigger:

* Weekly schedule

Steps:

1. Fetch all new leads for the week.
2. Group by readiness category.
3. Summarize common pain points.
4. Summarize top requested workflows.
5. Send internal weekly report.

This helps Solvin Solutions identify market demand and refine service offers.

---

### 13.6 Workflow 5: Case Study or Resource Follow-Up

Trigger:

* Recommendation category matches a specific service

Examples:

* Knowledge Assistant Ready → send article about internal knowledge assistants.
* Simple Automation Ready → send article about workflow automation before AI.
* Agent Workflow Candidate → send article about human-in-the-loop AI agents.

Steps:

1. Identify category.
2. Select matching resource.
3. Send follow-up email.
4. Log action.
